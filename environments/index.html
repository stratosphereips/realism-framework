Environments

A preliminary evaluation of 11 cyber environments across the 11 realism dimensions. Select an environment for its coverage radar, its fit by objective, and sources.

What the evaluation shows

Realism is use-case-relative: an environment can be well suited to one objective and unsuitable for another. Two families emerge — 5 real-software emulators cover the technical, attacker-facing dimensions, while 6 abstract simulators cover topology and the agent interface but abstract the surrounding context.

The dimensions most often absent across these environments are External information ecosystem, Temporal dynamics, Benign activity realism, OS fidelity, Telemetry fidelity — so objectives that depend on them are systematically under-served. In this catalog, no environment yet supports Targeted data exfiltration, Evasive red-team operation, Incident response, Threat hunting, Attacker-defender simulation: the objectives that most need those context dimensions.
