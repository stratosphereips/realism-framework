UC-S1 · Attacker-defender simulation

A system-level objective in which attacker and defender agents operate simultaneously and adapt to each other, as in purple-teaming and real engagements where both sides learn. The question is whether the defender can detect and contain the attacker, and how strategies co-evolve over repeated interaction. It requires a credible two-sided environment (both a real attacker action space and a real defender action-and-observation space), which is why environments that model only one side cannot support it.
