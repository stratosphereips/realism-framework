Realistic Enough for What?

Companion artifacts to the paper “Realistic Enough for What? A Multidimensional Framework for Evaluating Cyber Environments.” The framework asks not whether an environment is realistic in general, but whether it has the realism a given evaluation objective needs. Realism is decomposed into 11 dimensions (115 sub-dimensions), grouped by the part of an evaluation they affect.

The 11 realism dimensions

Infrastructure

Do the network, services, and hosts resemble those of a real technical system?

Topological realism
Network structure: segmentation, reachability, routing, and position-dependent visibility.
Service fidelity
Application-layer services that behave like real ones: protocols, versions, vulnerabilities, access controls, and served data.
OS fidelity
Realistic host operating-system behavior: processes, filesystems, persistence, privilege boundaries, and credential storage.

Organizational behavior

Does the environment contain realistic users, credentials, routine activity, and time-dependent behavior?

Identity realism
Users, credentials, groups, sessions, authentication protocols, MFA, federation, and authorization relationships.
Temporal dynamics
How time affects the environment: schedules, delays, session and token lifetimes, human rhythms, and long-horizon state.
Benign activity realism
Legitimate users, administrators, services, and background processes generating realistic routine activity, data, and noise.

Security layer

Does the environment model defensive controls and the security evidence they produce?

Defensive realism
Preventive, detective, hardening, deception, and response controls with realistic scope, latency, and side effects.
Telemetry fidelity
Security-relevant logs, alerts, flows, and artifacts with realistic schemas, noise, coverage gaps, and retention.

Agent interface

Does the agent act and observe through realistic capabilities and partial views?

Action realism
The agent's actions: realistic commands, APIs, tools, parameters, privileges, failures, costs, and consequences.
Observation realism
How the agent perceives the environment: partial, noisy, delayed, privilege-dependent views rather than ground truth.

External context

Does the environment model resources and dependencies outside the lab network?

External information ecosystem
Resources outside the lab network: OSINT, DNS, certificate transparency, public repositories, attacker infrastructure, cloud, and third-party dependencies.
